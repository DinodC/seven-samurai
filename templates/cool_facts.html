Fact number 1: The film was originally going to be called Six Samurai
- During the scriptwriting process, Kurosawa realised that six sober samurai were a bore and they needed a character that was more off-the-wall
- Kurosawa cast Mifune as Kikuchiyo and gave him creative license to improvise actions in his performance

Fact number 2: The film took a year to complete
- Toho Studios closed down production at least twice
- While Kurosawa calmly went fishing, he reasoned that the studio had already heavily invested in the production, and would allow him to complete the picture

Fact number 3: Akira spoke of ‘intense labour’ of making the film
- He reportedly said ‘It rained all the time, we didn’t have enough horses. It was just the kind of picture that is impossible to make in this country (Japan).’

Fact number 4: The martial arts choreography for the film was led by Yoshio Sugino of the Tenshin Shoden Katori Shinto-ryu

Fact number 5: At three hours, twenty-seven minutes, Seven Samurai would be the longest picture of Kurosawa’s career

Fact number 6: There have been pachinko machines based on Seven Samurai in Japan

Fact number 7: Seven Samurai’s influence can be felt on films such as The Magnificent Seven, A Bug’s Life, and Star Wars
